### Agenda

**1. Review Previous Sprint (5 min)**
- Review sprint goal and success criteria
- Review what was completed
- Discuss what was not completed and why
- Review metrics (velocity, quality, etc.)

**2. Review Product Backlog (15 min)**
- Product Owner presents top priority items
- Discuss large stories and break down
- Review acceptance criteria for stories
- Clarify questions from team

**3. Team Capacity Review (10 min)**
- Review team member availability
- Account for holidays, time off, training
- Calculate focus factor (typically 70-80%)
- Calculate team capacity in story points

**4. Select Stories for Sprint (20 min)**
- Team selects stories based on capacity and sprint goal
- Ensure stories meet Definition of Ready
- Break down stories into tasks if needed
- Identify dependencies and risks

**5. Estimate Stories (30 min)**
- Use Planning Poker for story estimation
- Discuss estimates and reach consensus
- Record estimates in backlog
- Note any technical spikes needed

**6. Define Sprint Goal (10 min)**
- Discuss what success looks like for this sprint
- Create measurable sprint goal
- Ensure goal aligns with product roadmap
- Get team commitment to sprint goal

**7. Identify Dependencies and Risks (10 min)**
- List internal dependencies between stories
- List external dependencies
- Identify risks and mitigation strategies
- Assign owners for external dependencies

## Meeting Norms

### Common Norms

1. **Start on Time, End on Time**
   - Respect everyone's time
   - Start within 2 minutes of scheduled time
   - End on time or agree to extend

2. **Be Prepared**
   - Review agenda beforehand
   - Complete pre-work before meeting
   - Have relevant data ready

3. **Active Participation**
   - Contribute to discussions
   - Share ideas and concerns
   - Ask clarifying questions

4. **Focus and Relevance**
   - Stay on topic
   - Avoid side conversations
   - Keep discussions relevant to all participants

5. **Constructive Feedback**
   - Be respectful and professional
   - Focus on process, not people
   - Provide suggestions for improvement

6. **Psychological Safety**
   - Safe environment to speak up
   - No blame or judgment
   - Encourage diverse perspectives
   - Welcome honest feedback

### Remote Meeting Norms

1. **Camera On**
   - Keep camera on during meetings
   - Use background blur if needed
   - Ensure good lighting and audio

2. **Minimize Distractions**
   - Mute notifications
   - Close unrelated tabs/apps
   - Use headphones to reduce background noise

3. **Use Collaboration Tools**
   - Use screen sharing for demos
   - Use chat for questions (avoid interrupting)
   - Record meetings for reference (with permission)
