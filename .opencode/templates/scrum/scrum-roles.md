## Product Owner (PO)

### Responsibilities

**Product Vision**:
- [ ] Owns and articulates product vision and strategy
- [ ] Aligns product vision with business goals and strategy
- [ ] Communicates vision to all stakeholders clearly

**Backlog Management**:
- [ ] Creates and maintains product backlog
- [ ] Prioritizes backlog items using MoSCoW (Must/Should/Could/Won't)
- [ ] Ensures backlog items are INVEST-compliant
- [ ] Refines backlog items with acceptance criteria
- [ ] Adjusts priorities based on changing business needs

**Stakeholder Management**:
- [ ] Represents product and team to stakeholders
- [ ] Gathers requirements from stakeholders
- [ ] Manages stakeholder expectations
- [ ] Provides transparent progress updates

**Acceptance and Validation**:
- [ ] Accepts or rejects completed work
- [ ] Validates that work meets business value
- [ ] Ensures acceptance criteria are met before marking done

**Roadmap Planning**:
- [ ] Creates and maintains product roadmap
- [ ] Plans releases and feature delivery
- [ ] Balances new features with maintenance
- [ ] Coordinates with other Product Owners (if applicable)

### Skills Required

**Business**:
- [ ] Domain expertise
- [ ] Market understanding
- [ ] Customer empathy
- [ ] Business case development
- [ ] ROI analysis

**Technical**:
- [ ] Technical feasibility understanding
- [ ] Architecture appreciation
- [ ] Dependency awareness

**Communication**:
- [ ] Clear communication skills
- [ ] Stakeholder management
- [ ] Negotiation skills
- [ ] Presentation skills
- [ ] Documentation skills

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Backlog is well-groomed and prioritized
- [ ] Clear acceptance criteria for all backlog items
- [ ] Stakeholders are satisfied with product direction
- [ ] Team understands requirements clearly
- [ ] Product is delivering business value

## Scrum Master (SM)

### Responsibilities

**Ceremony Facilitation**:
- [ ] Facilitates all Scrum ceremonies (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective)
- [ ] Ensures ceremonies are time-boxed and productive
- [ ] Creates safe environment for open communication
- [ ] Tracks and manages ceremony time

**Impediment Removal**:
- [ ] Identifies and removes impediments blocking team progress
- [ ] Escalates blockers that cannot be resolved at team level
- [ ] Tracks impediments and their resolution
- [ ] Proactively prevents future impediments

**Process Coaching**:
- [ ] Coaches team on Scrum practices and principles
- [ ] Encourages continuous improvement
- [ ] Helps team adhere to Definition of Done
- [ ] Facilitates retrospectives and ensures actions are completed

**Team Protection**:
- [ ] Protects team from external interruptions
- [ ] Manages scope changes during sprint
- [ ] Shields team from organizational pressure
- [ ] Ensures sustainable pace

**Metric Tracking**:
- [ ] Tracks team velocity and other metrics
- [ ] Reports on sprint progress and risks
- [ ] Identifies trends and areas for improvement
- [ ] Maintains transparency of team performance

### Skills Required

**Facilitation**:
- [ ] Strong facilitation skills
- [ ] Conflict resolution
- [ ] Time management
- [ ] Meeting management

**Coaching**:
- [ ] Coaching and mentoring experience
- [ ] Empathy and emotional intelligence
- [ ] Team development expertise
- [ ] Ability to give constructive feedback

**Communication**:
- [ ] Clear and effective communicator
- [ ] Active listening skills
- [ ] Diplomatic in handling conflicts
- [ ] Builds trust with team

**Process Knowledge**:
- [ ] Deep understanding of Scrum framework
- [ ] Knowledge of agile best practices
- [ ] Experience with different agile approaches
- [ ] Ability to adapt Scrum to context

### Success Criteria

- [ ] Scrum events are effective and well-attended
- [ ] Team understands and follows Scrum practices
- [ ] Impediments are identified and resolved quickly
- [ ] Team velocity is stable or improving
- [ ] Continuous improvement is happening

## Performance Expectations

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**For Product Owner**:
- [ ] Backlog readiness: >80% of top backlog items are DoR compliant
- [ ] Stakeholder satisfaction: >80% positive feedback
- [ ] Time to market: Features delivered within planned timeframes

**For Scrum Master**:
- [ ] Sprint success rate: >90% of sprints meet goals
- [ ] Impediment resolution time: <2 days for critical blockers
- [ ] Team satisfaction: >80% positive feedback
- [ ] Retrospective action completion: >90% actions completed

**For Development Team**:
- [ ] Velocity stability: Variation <10% between sprints
- [ ] Quality metrics: >80% test coverage, <2% defect escape rate
- [ ] Code review timeliness: PRs reviewed within [X] hours
- [ ] Definition of Done compliance: 100% of stories meet DoD

### Performance Review Process

**Frequency**: Quarterly
**Participants**: All roles, plus line management
**Process**:
1. Self-assessment by each individual
2. Peer feedback collected
3. 360-degree feedback (optional)
4. Manager review and calibration
5. Development plan created
6. Goals set for next period
