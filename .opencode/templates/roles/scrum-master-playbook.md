## Role Overview

The Scrum Master is accountable for establishing Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide. They help everyone understand Scrum theory and practice, both within the Scrum Team and the organization.

---

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Serve the Scrum Team

- [ ] **Coach the team** on self-management and cross-functionality
- [ ] **Facilitate Scrum events** as requested or needed
- [ ] **Remove impediments** to the team's progress
- [ ] **Ensure productivity** of Scrum events (positive, timeboxed)
- [ ] **Help with focus** on creating high-value Increments

### 2. Serve the Product Owner

- [ ] **Help find techniques** for effective backlog management
- [ ] **Help establish** empirical product planning
- [ ] **Facilitate stakeholder collaboration** as needed
- [ ] **Ensure Product Backlog** clarity and understanding

### 3. Serve the Organization

- [ ] **Lead and coach** the organization in Scrum adoption
- [ ] **Help employees and stakeholders** understand Scrum
- [ ] **Remove barriers** between stakeholders and Scrum Teams
- [ ] **Plan and advise** on Scrum implementations

---

## Daily Activities

### Morning
- [ ] Review team's standup notes/blockers from previous day
- [ ] Check sprint progress and burndown
- [ ] Prepare for day's meetings and coaching sessions

### During the Day
- [ ] Facilitate Daily Scrum (15 min)
- [ ] Work on removing impediments
- [ ] Coach team members as needed
- [ ] Coordinate with other Scrum Masters (if applicable)

### Weekly Activities
- [ ] Backlog refinement facilitation
- [ ] Impediment log review and updates
- [ ] One-on-ones with team members
- [ ] Scrum of Scrums (if applicable)

## Sprint Event Facilitation

### Sprint Planning (2 hours for 2-week sprint)

**Before**:
- [ ] Ensure backlog is refined with top items ready
- [ ] Confirm team availability and capacity
- [ ] Prepare room/virtual setup

**During**:
- [ ] Timebox the meeting
- [ ] Ensure Product Owner presents sprint goal
- [ ] Facilitate story discussion and commitment
- [ ] Help team break down work
- [ ] Capture sprint goal and sprint backlog

**After**:
- [ ] Ensure sprint backlog is visible
- [ ] Send sprint summary to stakeholders

### Daily Scrum (15 minutes)

**Before**:
- [ ] Ensure meeting starts on time
- [ ] Note any blockers from previous day

**During**:
- [ ] Keep meeting focused and timeboxed
- [ ] Listen for blockers
- [ ] Don't problem-solve during standup

**After**:
- [ ] Follow up on blockers immediately
- [ ] Update impediment log

### Sprint Review (1 hour for 2-week sprint)

**Before**:
- [ ] Confirm stakeholder attendance
- [ ] Prepare demo environment
- [ ] Review completed work with team

**During**:
- [ ] Facilitate demos (not present yourself)
- [ ] Capture stakeholder feedback
- [ ] Ensure backlog updates are noted

**After**:
- [ ] Document feedback
- [ ] Ensure backlog is updated

### Sprint Retrospective (1 hour for 2-week sprint)

**Before**:
- [ ] Choose retrospective format
- [ ] Prepare materials (boards, stickies, etc.)
- [ ] Review previous action items

**During**:
- [ ] Create safe environment
- [ ] Facilitate blameless discussion
- [ ] Help team identify actionable improvements
- [ ] Ensure action items have owners

**After**:
- [ ] Document action items
- [ ] Track action item completion

## Facilitation Techniques

### Retrospective Formats

**Start/Stop/Continue**
- What should we START doing?
- What should we STOP doing?
- What should we CONTINUE doing?

**Mad/Sad/Glad**
- What made you MAD?
- What made you SAD?
- What made you GLAD?

**4 L's**
- What did we LIKE?
- What did we LEARN?
- What did we LACK?
- What did we LONG FOR?

**Sailboat**
- Wind (what's pushing us forward)
- Anchor (what's holding us back)
- Rocks (risks ahead)
- Island (our goal)

### Conflict Resolution

1. **Acknowledge** the conflict exists
2. **Understand** all perspectives
3. **Find common ground**
4. **Collaborate** on solutions
5. **Follow up** to ensure resolution

### Team Building Activities

- [ ] Team retrospective games
- [ ] Pair programming rotations
- [ ] Knowledge sharing sessions
- [ ] Team lunch/social events
- [ ] Hackathons or innovation days

## Team Health Indicators

### Signs of a Healthy Team
- ✅ Team is self-organizing
- ✅ Velocity is stable
- ✅ Sprint commitments are met
- ✅ Retrospective actions are completed
- ✅ Team conflicts are resolved constructively
- ✅ Technical debt is managed
- ✅ Quality is maintained

### Signs of Team Dysfunction
- ❌ Missing sprint commitments regularly
- ❌ Increasing technical debt
- ❌ Silent retrospectives
- ❌ Finger-pointing and blame
- ❌ Low morale and engagement
- ❌ Dependencies on specific individuals
- ❌ Scope creep during sprints

## Coaching Techniques

### GROW Model
- **G**oal: What do you want to achieve?
- **R**eality: What's the current situation?
- **O**ptions: What could you do?
- **W**ill: What will you do?

### Powerful Questions
- "What would happen if...?"
- "How might you approach this differently?"
- "What's the smallest step you could take?"
- "What support do you need?"
- "What's holding you back?"

### When to Coach vs. Direct
| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| New team member | More directing |
| Experienced team | More coaching |
| Crisis situation | Direct, then coach |
| Learning opportunity | Coach |
| Repeated issue | Investigate, then coach |

## Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### ❌ Chairman Scrum Master
**Problem**: Runs meetings like corporate status meetings
**Impact**: Team disengagement
**Solution**: Facilitate, don't dictate

### ❌ Superhero Scrum Master
**Problem**: Solves all problems for the team
**Impact**: Team doesn't learn, dependency created
**Solution**: Coach team to solve their own problems

### ❌ Secretary Scrum Master
**Problem**: Only takes notes and schedules meetings
**Impact**: No coaching, no impediment removal
**Solution**: Actively coach and protect the team

### ❌ Police Scrum Master
**Problem**: Enforces Scrum rules dogmatically
**Impact**: Team resentment, surface compliance
**Solution**: Explain the "why" behind practices

### ❌ Part-time Scrum Master
**Problem**: Has other full-time responsibilities
**Impact**: Insufficient support for team
**Solution**: Dedicated Scrum Master role
